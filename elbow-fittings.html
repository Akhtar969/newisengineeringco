What Are Elbow Fittings (45° & 90°)?

Elbow Fittings are pipe fittings used to change the direction of flow by the specified angle — either 45° (eighth bend) or 90° (quarter bend). The elbow connects two pipes at its two ends, redirecting the flow in the new direction.

Elbow fittings are specified by three key parameters: angle (45° or 90°), radius type (Short Radius or Long Radius), and nominal bore size (NB). The centreline radius of the elbow determines the smoothness of the flow change and the pressure drop through the fitting.

Short Radius (SR) vs Long Radius (LR) Elbows — Key Differences

  • Short Radius (SR / 1D): Centreline radius = 1× pipe NB. More compact. Higher pressure drop. Used where space is limited. Not recommended for gas/slurry service or where pressure drop is critical.
  • Long Radius (LR / 1.5D): Centreline radius = 1.5× pipe NB. Smoother flow change. Lower pressure drop and turbulence. Standard for process piping. Preferred in ASME B31.3 process piping.

Elbow Fittings (45° & 90°) – Industries & Applications

Oil & Gas Piping

90° LR elbows used at all direction changes in refinery process piping, gas pipelines and petrochemical plant piping. LR elbows preferred to minimise erosion and pressure drop in gas and liquid hydrocarbon service.

Water Supply & Plumbing

Used in municipal water mains, building plumbing and domestic water supply at all direction changes. Both SR and LR elbows used — LR for main pipework, SR for tight plumbing spaces.

Chemical & Process Plants

LR elbows standard in chemical process piping to minimise turbulence, erosion and pressure drop. Important in corrosive service where wall thinning at SR elbows can be rapid.

Fire-Fighting Systems

Used in fire-fighting ring mains, sprinkler systems and hydrant networks at direction changes. Both SR and LR elbows used depending on available space and pressure drop requirements.

Construction & Infrastructure

Used in HVAC systems, building plumbing, sewage lines and utility piping at all changes of direction. Most common fittings in construction site fabrication and building services engineering.

Marine & Shipbuilding

Used in ship piping systems for ballast, bilge, fuel, fire-fighting and utility service. SR elbows preferred in ship machinery spaces where space is extremely limited.

What are Elbow Pipe Fittings?

+

What is the difference between Short Radius and Long Radius Elbows?

+

Short Radius (SR) Elbows: Centreline radius = 1× nominal pipe size (1D). More compact but higher pressure drop and more flow turbulence. Used in tight spaces where layout demands compact fittings. Long Radius (LR) Elbows: Centreline radius = 1.5× nominal pipe size (1.5D). Smoother flow change with lower pressure drop and turbulence. Standard specification for most process, oil & gas and water piping. LR elbows are preferred unless space specifically requires SR.

When should I use a 45° Elbow vs a 90° Elbow?

+

Use a 90° Elbow when the pipe must turn at a right angle (perpendicular direction change). Use a 45° Elbow when the pipe must turn at a 45° angle — often used in pairs to create an offset or route pipes around obstructions at lower pressure drop than a 90° elbow. Two 45° elbows create a 90° offset with less turbulence than a single 90° elbow.
