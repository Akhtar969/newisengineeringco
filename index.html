What are M.S. Flanges and what are they used for?

+

M.S. (Mild Steel) Flanges are flat, disc-shaped mechanical components manufactured from mild steel (IS:2062 grade). They are used to connect pipes, valves, pumps and equipment in industrial piping systems, providing a reliable, pressure-tight and easily dismantlable bolted joint. Types include Blind Flanges, Welding Neck Flanges, Slip-On Flanges, Socket Weld Flanges, Forged Flanges and E Table Flanges. They are widely used in oil & gas, petrochemical, power, construction, water supply and marine industries.

What are E Table Flanges? What is IS:1538 Table E?

+

E Table Flanges are flanges manufactured as per Indian Standard IS:1538 Table E. IS:1538 is the Indian Standard for cast iron and mild steel pipe fittings and flanges. The "Table E" dimension series specifies the outside diameter (OD), bolt circle diameter (PCD), number of bolt holes, bolt diameter and flange thickness for each nominal bore size from 15mm to 600mm. E Table Flanges use a flat face (FF) design and are available in pressure ratings PN6 (6 bar), PN10 (10 bar) and PN16 (16 bar). They are the standard flange specification for Indian municipal water supply, sewage, fire-fighting, irrigation and industrial process piping.

What is the difference between E Table Flanges and standard M.S. Flanges?

+

Standard M.S. Flanges can be manufactured to various international standards (ANSI B16.5, BS 4504, DIN 2573), while E Table Flanges specifically conform to Indian Standard IS:1538 Table E. The key differences are: E Table Flanges use flat face (FF) mating surface, IS-specified bolt circle and bolt hole dimensions; they are designed for compatibility with IS-standard valves, pumps and fittings used in Indian infrastructure projects. For Indian municipal, water authority and public sector projects, IS:1538 E Table Flanges are mandatory.

What is the difference between Blind Flanges and Welding Neck Flanges?

+

Blind Flanges are solid discs (no bore hole) used to blank off or terminate the end of a pipe, nozzle or valve — acting as a pipe end cap. They are ideal for pressure testing and future pipeline extensions. Welding Neck Flanges have a long tapered neck that is butt-welded directly to the pipe, distributing stress uniformly — making them the preferred choice for high-pressure, high-temperature and cyclic service piping in oil & gas refineries, power plants and petrochemical plants.

What is the difference between Concentric and Eccentric Reducer Fittings?

+

Concentric Reducers are symmetrical — both pipe centrelines are aligned at the same axis. They are used in vertical piping or where centreline alignment is required. Eccentric Reducers have one flat side — the top face remains flat (flat side up at pump suctions to prevent air pocket formation; flat side down at pump discharges). Eccentric reducers are used on horizontal piping where drainage is important or at centrifugal pump suctions.

What is the difference between SORF, WNRF and BLRF flanges?

+

SORF (Slip-On Raised Face) Flanges slide over the pipe and are welded on both sides — economical, easy to install, ideal for low-to-medium pressure. WNRF (Welding Neck Raised Face) Flanges are butt-welded to the pipe via a tapered neck — the strongest type, preferred for high-pressure and high-temperature service in oil & gas and petrochemical plants. BLRF (Blind Raised Face) Flanges are solid discs used to close off a pipe end — used for pressure testing and future pipeline extensions. I.S. Engineering Co. manufactures all three types in Mumbai.
