Product FAQ

What is the difference between E Table Flanges and standard M.S. Flanges?

+

Standard M.S. Flanges can be made to various international standards (ANSI B16.5, BS 4504, DIN). E Table Flanges (IS:1538 Table E) follow the Indian Standard IS:1538 dimension series specifically — with Indian-specified OD, bolt circle, bolt count and flange thickness for each NB size. E Table Flanges are mandated for Indian municipal water supply, sewage, fire-fighting and public sector piping projects. For projects specifying IS standard flanges, E Table Flanges are the correct choice.

What is the difference between Concentric and Eccentric Reducer Fittings?

+

Concentric Reducers: Both inlet and outlet pipe centrelines are on the same axis. Used in vertical piping or horizontal piping where centreline alignment is required. Eccentric Reducers: One side (flat side) is straight while the other is tapered. At centrifugal pump suctions, installed flat-side-up to prevent air pocket formation. At pump discharges, installed flat-side-down for proper drainage. Always specify concentric or eccentric when ordering reducer fittings.

What is the difference between Short Radius and Long Radius Elbow Fittings?

+

Short Radius (SR) Elbow: Centreline radius = 1× the nominal pipe size (1D). More compact, used in tight spaces but causes higher pressure drop. Long Radius (LR) Elbow: Centreline radius = 1.5× the nominal pipe size (1.5D). Provides a smoother, gradual change of direction with lower pressure drop and flow turbulence. Long radius elbows are preferred in most process piping systems. Short radius elbows are used where space is the primary constraint.
