What Are MS Square Pipes (SHS)? – Direct Manufacturer in Mumbai

M.S. Square Hollow Section (SHS) Pipes — also called Mild Steel Square Tubes or MS Box Sections — are hollow structural steel sections with equal width and height dimensions (square cross-section). They are manufactured by the Electric Resistance Welding (ERW) process from hot-rolled mild steel strip to IS:4923 standard.

Square hollow sections are among the most versatile structural steel products — combining excellent compressive strength, torsional rigidity and a clean aesthetic appearance. Their equal wall thickness on all four sides provides uniform strength in all directions, making them ideal for columns, frames and structural supports where loading direction is uncertain.

MS Square Pipes (SHS) – Frequently Asked Questions (Mumbai Manufacturer)

What are M.S. Square Pipes (SHS)?

+

M.S. Square Hollow Section (SHS) Pipes are hollow structural steel tubes with equal width and height cross-section, manufactured from mild steel to IS:4923 standard. Also called MS Square Tubes or Box Sections. Available in sizes 10×10mm to 250×250mm with wall thickness 1.0mm to 12mm.

What is IS:4923 standard for Square Pipes?

+

IS:4923 is the Indian Standard for Hollow Steel Sections for Structural Use. It specifies the dimensional tolerances, mechanical properties, and testing requirements for Square Hollow Sections (SHS) and Rectangular Hollow Sections (RHS) manufactured in India. IS:4923 SHS pipes from I.S. Engineering Co. conform fully to this standard.

What sizes of M.S. Square Pipes are available?

+

I.S. Engineering Co. manufactures M.S. Square Pipes in sizes from 10×10mm to 250×250mm with wall thickness 1.0mm to 12mm. Standard lengths available are 3m and 6m. Custom lengths and cut-to-size options are available for bulk orders. Popular sizes in stock: 25×25, 40×40, 50×50, 75×75, 100×100mm.

What is the difference between M.S. Square Pipes and M.S. Round Pipes?

+

Square Hollow Sections (SHS) provide equal strength in all directions, have flat surfaces for easy bolting and welding, and offer a cleaner aesthetic — preferred for visible structural work, gates, furniture and machine frames. Round Hollow Sections (RHS/CHS) provide higher torsional strength per unit weight and are preferred for fluid transport, structural columns in circular arrangements and architectural applications.
