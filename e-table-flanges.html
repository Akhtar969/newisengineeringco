E Table Flanges IS:1538 – Full Product Details

What Are E Table Flanges?

E Table Flanges (also written as IS 1538 Table E Flanges or IS:1538 Table-E Flanges) are Mild Steel pipe flanges manufactured as per Indian Standard IS:1538 Table E. They are the most widely used flange type in India for domestic, municipal and industrial piping projects where Indian Standard compliance is mandatory.

The term "Table E" refers to a specific dimension table within the IS:1538 standard. This table defines, for each nominal bore (NB) size: the flange outside diameter (OD), the bolt circle diameter (PCD), the number of bolt holes, the bolt hole diameter, and the minimum flange thickness. All these dimensions are specified uniquely for Indian piping systems — making IS:1538 Table E flanges directly compatible with Indian valves, pumps, pipe fittings and other IS-standard flanges.

E Table Flanges feature a flat face (FF) mating surface. When bolted together with a full-face gasket, the flat faces provide a reliable, pressure-tight seal across the full flange face area — making them ideal for water, sewage and low-to-medium pressure industrial service.

Important: Selecting the Correct PN Rating

Always select a pressure rating higher than your system's maximum allowable working pressure (MAWP). Include a safety margin — if your system MAWP is 8 bar, specify PN10 minimum. For fluctuating pressure or water hammer-prone systems, go one rating higher.

What are E Table Flanges?

+

E Table Flanges are Mild Steel pipe flanges manufactured as per Indian Standard IS:1538 Table E. They define specific dimensional parameters (OD, PCD, bolt count, bolt size, thickness) for each nominal bore size from 15mm to 600mm. They feature a flat face (FF) mating surface and are available in pressure ratings PN6, PN10 and PN16. They are the standard flange specification for Indian municipal water, sewage, fire-fighting and industrial piping projects.

What is the IS:1538 Table E standard for flanges?

+

IS:1538 is the Indian Standard specification for cast iron and mild steel pipe flanges. It contains multiple dimension tables (Table D, Table E, Table F, etc.) each defining different dimensional series. Table E is the most widely used dimension series in India for water and industrial piping. The Table E series specifies flanges that are interchangeable with valves, fittings and equipment manufactured to IS:1538 by all major Indian manufacturers.

What pressure ratings are available for E Table Flanges?

+

IS:1538 E Table Flanges are available in three pressure ratings: PN6 (6 bar / ~87 PSI) for water distribution mains; PN10 (10 bar / ~145 PSI) for industrial process piping; PN16 (16 bar / ~232 PSI) for oil, gas and higher pressure industrial service. Select a pressure rating above your system's maximum allowable working pressure (MAWP) for safety.

What is the difference between E Table Flanges and standard ANSI flanges?

+

E Table Flanges (IS:1538) follow Indian Standard dimensions — including OD, bolt circle, bolt count and thickness for each NB size. They use a flat face (FF) design. ANSI Flanges (ANSI B16.5) follow American Standard dimensions — different OD, PCD, bolt count values for the same NB sizes. They typically have a raised face (RF). The two standards are NOT interchangeable — do not mix IS:1538 and ANSI flanges in the same bolted joint. Always match the standard of the flange to the standard of the mating valve or fitting.

What gasket should be used with E Table Flanges?

+

IS:1538 E Table Flanges have a flat face (FF) mating surface. They must always be used with a full-face (FF) gasket that covers the entire flange face including the bolt holes. Common gasket materials for E Table Flanges include: natural rubber (NR) for water; neoprene (CR) for oil service; EPDM for chemical service; compressed asbestos fibre (CAF) or graphite-based sheet for general industrial service. Never use a raised face (RF) ring gasket with flat face flanges.
